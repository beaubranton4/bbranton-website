June 22, 2026

I can't stop thinking about hats. Specifically how they fit — or how they don't.

My head is just an odd shape. It's really small, and almost 95% of hats don't fit me. But I've loved wearing hats ever since I was a kid. As a baseball player my whole identity was kind of "the guy who loved wearing hats." I've got 200, maybe 300 hats. And the crazy thing is, not many of them actually look good on me. So when I find one that does, I latch onto it and wear it into the ground.

Here's the thing that's been bugging me. A hat looks good from the front when I check the mirror — but then I see a photo of myself from the side, and there's all this space at the top and the back of the crown that I just wish would collapse down onto my head. King & Fifth is the closest I've found to solving it; they basically scale the crown height to the circumference of the hat. That's exactly the right instinct, and that's what needs to happen. But even then, it's not the perfect fit.

So I'm wondering: is there demand for some kind of technology solution that gets the perfect fit — a custom-fit hat? Why are hats so one-size-fits-all? Pants, shirts — those get tailored to you, or at least come in three or four or five different sizes. But with most hats it's just one size fits most, with a strap in the back. Why is that?

I know custom-fit hats would be costly to make. But I think people would buy them. I would. I'd pay $100 for an amazing, well-fitting hat that I love and wear over and over. Maybe even a subscription — I'd pay $500 a year to get a new perfectly-fitting hat whenever I want, or a rotating wardrobe, a hat a month. The tricky part is you've got to try it on, because everyone likes different styles and has different preferences. But the fit problem is real, it's mine, and I can't let it go.
