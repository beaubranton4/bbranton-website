# March 27, 2026

A lot of the most productive moments that I have are the moments when I sit and just think about what I want and how I can go about getting there.

To give a couple of simple, not-that-deep examples: I want a certain haircut. I take a look at my hair, I look at pictures of when my hair looked that certain way or other hairstyles, and I say, "Okay, my hair is not long enough. I need to grow my hair." Then I decide, "Okay, I don't have to cut my hair." It breaks you out of the habit of, "Hey, I need a haircut every month."

A lot of things we do in life are just going through the motions, doing what we're instinctually and habitually programmed to do. A lot of that can be reprogrammed simply by taking the time to think, "What is it that I really, really want, and how do I get there?"

I think too often in life we are kind of going about things without a plan, especially myself. I'm someone who is very adept at going with the flow. I love being chill, riding the wave, right? And in many ways that's benefited me. But the problem with not having a destination in mind is that you wander. Like a drunk kid at a frat party, you wander.

Being intentional about taking time every single day to think and outline your goals is a practice that I have found I absolutely cannot live without. It has allowed me to quit things like nicotine just through the habit of writing it down every single day. It took some time, but I believe that I'm firmly off of it and not addicted to nicotine anymore. I still indulge occasionally. But the habit of writing this goal down every single day, writing it down in my 10-year plan, in my yearly plan, and in my weekly plan, it came to fruition.

What do I need to do to achieve that? Well, it's simple. Just don't do it, right? But there are other goals that do require a little bit more action and planning.

Goals like, "Hey, I want to be an entrepreneur," but we need to be more specific. Because just writing that goal down every single day, of course it's going to give you an overwhelmingly large, looming north star to follow, but it's not going to help when you get down to, "How do I spend the next three hours?"

So continually refining those goals and thinking about, "Okay, I want to be an entrepreneur. I should probably find an idea."

One of the most productive things I did was, two years ago or a year ago, brainstorm all of the realm of possible projects that I could pursue. I narrowed it down to the lineup generator and Dugout Edge, and that led me to building this tool and this platform that now has over 100 customers. I should continue building and working down that path.

It's me questioning every morning, "Should I work on this?" And the answer is overwhelmingly "yes," many days in a row. So I continue to work on it.

All that to say, I need to continue with this daily meditation, this daily practice of speaking, just talking my thoughts out loud, writing down what I'm thinking, and writing down my goals. That way it's not like my day is 100% prescribed, but I have a purpose every single day, and that purpose kind of guides me.

Sometimes I'll stray from that path. Most of the time I don't accomplish what I want to get done. But all in all, it's one of the most important things for me.
