May 19, 2026

Back home now. Beautiful day in San Francisco. House painting's finished. Getting our new garage installed, new lanterns, new windows. Excited to see our new house. We might have spent a little bit too much money, but that's just how it goes.

Going back to Hawaii — Nick and Jess's wedding was this weekend. Incredible time. At Turtle Bay. Got to hang out with old friends, put on for the buddies, meet some new friends, hang out at a five-star hotel. Slightly overrated, but honestly, great experience. Played some pickleball, hung out by the pool, played some golf.

Figured some nice things out with my swing, by the way. Just standing straight up, thinking of a nice baby cut. Scotty Scheffler. A lot more vertical with my swing, and I think it's helping.

Emotional wedding. I don't know, love is amazing. Love is beautiful. Love is fun to watch. And it makes you think of yourself, your own love. It's just emotional. I love how weddings make you feel that way. I've never been to a bad wedding. Never been to a bad wedding.

Travel day yesterday. Worked.

Mom canceled her appointment to see a doctor. I think she's just afraid of getting diagnosed with something and then making that feel real — that it's official that something is wrong with her. I think she just needs some time to process that. Maybe some of her family members are feeling that way too, other than my dad. I do look forward to moving that process along and helping her out, helping us out. Because I think it just needs to be addressed, really.

Time to really lock in on my health, my fitness, my goals. Wedding is nine months away. I think that's a really good milestone for me to say: life is serious. I'm not a kid anymore. It's time to grow up. While we can still play, we can still have fun. Time is limited. February 6th, 2027 is going to be a really good milestone for me to work towards and maybe actually take some of the real actions that I know I need to get done — even though I really know I need to be doing that stuff today, right now.
