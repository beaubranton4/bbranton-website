June 10, 2026

So it was confirmed by my mom's neurologist that she has Alzheimer's. Which is not new news, but it's good to get confirmation from the doctor himself. They took a brain scan and did a blood test and found TAU, which is a sign of Alzheimer's, as well as showing some dark matter in the brain, basically showing some gaps where brain matter should be.

But it is NCI, which is I believe the term for early stage Alzheimer's. The next step is to do a spinal tap to get confirmation of amyloid in the brain and look for, I think it was like p2TAU, pTAU 217. We can basically confirm whether or not she is a good candidate for lecanemab. So, you know, fingers crossed that it's not too late. Fingers crossed that it's too early, because that would be even better. She's okay for the time being.

Yeah. I spent the last 48 hours of work just thinking through a metric definition — not only how do we define the metric, but how do we associate that metric and roll it up to the many different levels that you can aggregate the metric by. Requires, you know, different conversations with different stakeholders, different tradeoffs when visualizing it in different ways, relationships to other metrics, and how there needs to be consistency reporting at every grain — session, test, subscription, account. It's such a problem.

I'm not sure how to solve that problem, but it seems like one worth fixing, but also one that I'm sure every single BI tool is after. But I don't know how many of them are actually concerned with the tool for standardizing these sorts of things. We almost need a sort of ledger, a source of truth for all of these different decisions on how things get reported and how numbers get rolled up, etc., etc.

I'm excited to try out the new Fable model. I'm not sure what to point it at. Should I point it at something huge like build me a new website that almost replicates Dugout Edge to a T that is just made for basketball or is made for soccer. The same concept, the same premise, the same values — save coaches time. The only difference is that, you know, the content needs to be catered towards soccer coaches and soccer players. The tools need to be rebuilt to match how soccer games work, playing time works, all of those sorts of decisions.

Do I ask it to build out a new feature for Dugout Edge? Maybe I ask it to completely redo GameChanger or replicate GameChanger. See how well it does there. Or maybe I just point it at an existing task that I've been working on. I'm not sure.
