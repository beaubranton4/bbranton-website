June 2, 2026

Dugout Edge has been losing users. The last week of May was a pretty bad week in terms of churn and number of signups — I probably had less than a dozen signups and probably just as many users churned, for the first time in the last three months. The number of active subscribers has been flat week over week, but I'm starting to see another pickup this week. So my guess is that a lot of seasons end at the end of May. Summer ball starts to pick up beginning of June. Hopefully this trend holds strong all the way throughout fall ball. If not, that's how it is, but it's great to get one year's worth of data.

I've started to collect feedback from my users via email. The underlying mechanic was through a Resend agent and an automated manual text blast — that's "redundant automated manual." Basically I built out the email and then automated the send via cron jobs because of my Resend limits. I probably should just pay for the premium tier of Resend, but I'm way too cheap to do so. Trying to keep my costs to a minimum.

Let's see — 200 divided by 8.20. That's about 25 customers, which is about right. We'll call it 10% of my customers, so my margins are about 10% a month, which is really good. Really, really good. So I think I have some room to spend. That's basically what I'm trying to say. I need to get more comfortable with spending. I can't just keep everything as profit and put it in my pocket, especially this early in the business.

Maybe I should challenge myself to get to 60% gross margins. That way I assure myself that I'm investing in the business and saving time. Maybe I can continue a conversation with Claude on that. Another big way I can invest is just a one-time investment in a content creator, or maybe someone to help me build out the app — but that's the part that I enjoy, so maybe not. I do think a $5,000 investment to get some influencers might be a really smart move, but maybe I wait until next February or March to start doing so. In either case, I can't just pocket all of it. I have to reinvest into the business to let it grow.

I spent a lot of time yesterday applying to a data analyst role at Anthropic. It was an analytics role for their finance and strategy team. I think I'm well qualified for it, but the one thing I'm not sure about is just my pedigree. I don't know if my resume is strong enough to appeal, and I know they are the most sought-after company right now. Everybody wants to work for Anthropic, so I shouldn't get my hopes up. I'm playing against very, very high competition.

And at the same time, my goal is to become an entrepreneur, so I can't lose track of that. I don't want to lose sight of that. Applying to jobs, thinking about my career, thinking about my resume, thinking about data and analytics — in my personal opinion, it is a waste of time. So I need to remember that.

---

Lastly, I have one really good idea. I've seen a couple of content creators build a Jarvis demo on TikTok and the engagement is insane. The dude is just standing behind his computer saying, "Hey Jarvis, open up my Slack. How did our business perform today?" It's a 20-second video and it looks really awesome. He's got like six monitors up, he's got the spinning thing, Jarvis speaks in a nice British accent, it opens up things on his computer, it types things. We all know it's a demo.

But I think we're getting really close to an interface where we can interact with our computer just through spoken word — just like I'm talking to you right now. But instead of just recording text, we're taking action, and we're doing that instantaneously in real time.

I think there are many, many challenges with building out a solution like that. So I think if I were to get involved in this space, it would be a really good idea, and that's something that could grow to become a hundred-million-dollar-a-year business. I think I need to start in a specific subniche, or start with a specific problem. Maybe that problem is browser use, or maybe that problem is dictation, or maybe I solve the issues for a specific vertical. I start with my to-do list app and I make that one workflow controllable by voice.

Journaling and to-do list. Journaling and to-do list.
