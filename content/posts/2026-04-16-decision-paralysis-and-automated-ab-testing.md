# April 16, 2026

One of the biggest issues people face in the corporate world is making decisions. They go into analysis paralysis. So much of corporate money is spent to offload the stress of making a decision. What product feature do we create next? Who do we hire? Do we charge 30 or 50? What products do we sell?

In a world where we can do anything, build anything, the world of possibilities is a trillion gajillion permutations. It's one of the reasons why UserTesting is a good business. People need to get feedback from real people to inform decisions. The problem is that everyone has different opinions, and many times a lot of feedback can just turn into even more noise.

I wonder in this agentic world where building anything is extremely easy, if the answer is to simply A/B test everything. It's so easy to build, why don't we just test things in parallel and build it in an instance, deploy it, track the analytics, and choose the winner. What if all that was automated and done for you?

This tool would surface all the things it would recommend A/B testing. It would design and show you all the different ways to test (or customize your own design). It would launch, deploy, and analyze, and then you can auto launch iteratively or choose.

It's as simple as just multiple-choice choosing what you want. Interesting concept...
