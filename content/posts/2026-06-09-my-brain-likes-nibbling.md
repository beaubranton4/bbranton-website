June 9, 2026

I've come to realize that my brain really likes nibbling on complex problems. The type of problems where there's no right answer. There's an entire multitude of answers that you could arrive on, all with complex moving parts, all with their own pros and cons and trade-offs.

And for better or for worse, my mind likes to noodle on every single possibility, weigh the pros and cons, take the time, think through all of them inside and out, and then make the decision. These decisions can span hours, days, weeks, even months. While they're the most frustrating problems to encounter, they're also the most satisfying to solve.

For example, the dilemma at work regarding how we report on sessions, tests, and subscriptions and how they all roll up to each other. It's just one example. I've spent the last 24 hours thinking about it.

I wonder if I'm just dumb. Other people might just simplify and make a decision. If I'm smart, because I think through every single option. Either way, my brain likes nibbling.

The secret sauce for me is patience. I can nibble patiently until I eat the whole pie.
