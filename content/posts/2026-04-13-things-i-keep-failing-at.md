# April 13, 2026

I asked Claude to read every journal entry I've written since August 2025 — all 185 of them — and find the things I keep saying I need to do but never actually do.

This is in an effor to find a problem worth solving for myself and for others.

Something to dedicate my being to.

---

## 1. Marketing and content creation *(~45–50 entries)*

This is the biggest one. It shows up in roughly half of all my entries.

I know distribution matters more than features. I've said it over and over. I've watched other people build audiences and wished I had one. I know that SEO alone has a ceiling and that I need to put myself out there in video, social, email — something.

And I don't do it.

I've diagnosed the reason myself: coding feels safe, marketing feels exposed. When I ship a feature, the product gets judged. When I post a video, *I* get judged. My thoughts, my framing, my face. That vulnerability has been stopping me for eight months.

The phrase I keep coming back to: "I hate selling, marketing, or hyping myself."

That's the wall. And I haven't climbed it.

---

## 2. Drinking less *(~35–40 entries)*

I said on January 1st that this would be my hardest 2026 goal. I committed to drinking fewer than 36 times all year.

I still don't know where I stand against that number, but based on the entries, I'm not on pace.

The pattern is always the same: great week, strong habits, then Friday hits. A couple drinks become a few. Sleep suffers. Saturday is a wash. Sunday is recovery. Monday I restart.

I've connected this clearly to everything else — bad sleep, brain fog, skipped workouts, junk food, broken streaks. It's not that drinking is the only problem. It's that it's the domino that knocks everything else over.

I've written the same entry about this probably 35 times.

---

## 3. Consistent exercise *(~40–45 entries)*

I do work out. This isn't about being sedentary. But I can't maintain the streak.

The pattern: two or three good weeks, then a weekend, or travel, or illness, or just a bad day, breaks the chain. Then I spend the next week clawing back to where I was. The net progress is slow.

The knee injury in late 2025 was a setback I'm still partially dealing with. And I've been honest with myself that my workouts lately are more about going through the motions than about actually pushing.

One recurring theme: I spend an hour at the gym in the morning, feel drained, spend another hour in the afternoon, and then the whole day is just recovery. I need to work out *harder*, not longer. More intensity, less volume.

---

## 4. Fixing my posture *(~15–18 entries)*

I've said this has taken up five years of mental space. I believe my neck and upper back tightness is connected to anxiety, shallow breathing, and a general sense of physical unease that I carry around all day.

I sit at a computer all day. I know this. I've written about needing to stretch, to get away from the screen, to stand more, to move differently. It shows up in all-caps to-do lists. It shows up in birthday goal lists.

Still not fixed.

---

## 5. Selling — actually learning how to sell *(~30–35 entries)*

Building is comfortable. Selling requires you to put a price on something you made and convince someone else it's worth that price. There's rejection in selling. There's ego in selling.

I've identified this as a critical skill gap since the very first weeks of this journal. I've said I need to learn how to sell, or find a co-founder who can, or hire someone. None of those things have happened.

Right now Dugout Edge grows through SEO. That's a passive channel. It doesn't require me to talk to anyone. I've been hiding behind it.

---

## 6. Morning routine and daily structure *(~25–30 entries)*

I've written out my ideal morning probably a dozen times. Slow start, no phone, some movement, time to think.

I rarely execute it for more than a few days in a row. The entries where I describe a great morning routine are almost always followed by entries where I've gone "a little laissez-faire" or "lost the thread."

The problem isn't knowing what the routine should look like. The problem is that I keep treating it as something I'll restart tomorrow.

---

## 7. Diet and body fat *(~25–30 entries)*

This goes hand-in-hand with drinking and exercise. The specific goal that's come up repeatedly in 2026 is getting to 12% body fat, which means actually tracking calories.

I've given this advice to Abby. I'm not taking it myself.

The cycle I've described: eat well Monday through Thursday, then the weekend comes and the wheels come off. Junk food, drinking, poor sleep, start over.

---

## 8. Reducing screen time / allowing more boredom *(~15–18 entries)*

I keep coming back to needing more silence. More boredom. Less phone, less scrolling, less input. I've written really clearly about how the thoughts in my head aren't even mine — they're just recycled content from Twitter and TikTok.

I know the fix: put the phone down, sit with nothing, let the mind wander. I've written about this enough times that clearly I'm not doing it.

---

## 9. Regular meditation *(~14 entries)*

I love it every time I do it. I've described sessions where I feel clear, calm, and like I actually know what I want. I've connected it to better breathing, better posture, and actually listening to myself.

I do not maintain it as a habit. It shows up as a one-off positive moment, not a practice.

## 10. Building a second brain / unified system *(~12–15 entries)*

I have notes scattered everywhere — Apple Notes, this journal, screenshots, voice memos, random files. I've described wanting one unified place for all of it: goals, ideas, to-dos, ChatGPT conversations, calendar, everything. I've even thought about building it myself.

It doesn't exist. The notes are still scattered. The goal keeps getting deferred.

## 11. Finding community *(~10–12 entries)*

I miss belonging to something. I've written about it pretty openly — the sense of being part of a team that I had in baseball and college, that I haven't had since. I've thought about joining a run club, an adult sports league, building some kind of weekly social thing myself.

I haven't joined anything. I haven't built anything. The isolation of remote work and solo building is real, and I keep acknowledging it without doing anything about it.

---

## 12. Investing my savings *(~4–6 entries)*

I have somewhere around $30–50K sitting uninvested. I've put this in all-caps to-do lists. I've thought about real estate, index funds, and other options. I've never reported taking action on it.

Money sitting in cash is a slow bleed. I know this. I'm not doing anything about it.

---

## The meta-pattern

Every single one of these has the same shape.

Monday through Thursday: good habits, focused work, healthy choices, strong intentions.

Friday through Sunday: drinking, junk food, broken routine, no marketing, no content, no selling.

Monday: restart.

The weekend is where everything goes to die. And alcohol is almost always the variable.

---

## What this tells me

I'm not failing because I don't know what to do. I know exactly what to do. I've written it down hundreds of times.

I'm failing because there's a version of me that wants the results but isn't willing to feel the discomfort required to get them. The discomfort of putting my face in a video. The discomfort of saying no to a beer on a Friday. The discomfort of feeling exposed, judged, or bored.

That's the actual problem. Everything else is just symptoms.
