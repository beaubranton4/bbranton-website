Caught this from the announcer during Argentina vs Algeria today and it really moved me:

> "For Algeria — two years ago this week, Nebul Dentalev had a heart attack as he was playing a kickaround with friends and family in France. He was placed in an induced coma. He was given a pacemaker. He missed eight months of action. And Nebul Dentalev, again, two years almost to the day, is now at his second World Cup. It is miraculous. It is remarkable. About the age of 31 to be out here experiencing this moment.
>
> It's an example of — you know, we do these games, and you've got 22 guys on the field and so many more on the bench, and each has lived this incredible, remarkable story to get to this moment. Some of them get their name in lights because they score a goal, or Bolzina the goalkeeper for Cape Verde makes a series of saves and becomes a social media sensation. For so many — victory is just being here."

Wonderful words about stories that really moved me.
