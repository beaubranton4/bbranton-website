June 6, 2026

Last week, this week, has been disappointing. It has been disappointing in the sense that I have lacked discipline. I have given in to temptation. And where this pains me the most is not in the fact that I didn't eat healthy. I didn't hit my goals. I had a few edibles, had a few zins. The reason this hurts is because it lowers how I view myself. When I look in the mirror, I have less respect for the person because of the decisions that I made, or rather the decisions that I did not make. What I was not able to resist, what I was not able to turn down. And that's where it pains me the most.

I continually justify it to myself by telling myself that, "Oh, well, you're so much better than last year. You're so much better than two years ago." And while that is 100% true, I am learning to moderate. I'm just not doing myself any favors by going back to those old habits, by giving in to those temptations.

Took edibles three times this week. Bought that can of zins two weekends ago, finished on Monday, took three days off, bought another one Friday. Right, so I'm just back. And I keep telling myself, "Well, it's so easy. Now that I know how to quit, I'm on and off, I'm on and off." Well, then why even do it in the first place? My perception of myself is that I'm someone who just bought zins again, and I can buy them again. And so while I've done so good this year, and I've more or less quit zins, the fact that I just bought some in the last two means that I haven't, and I can keep telling myself whatever I want, but that's the truth of the matter.

Same with the edibles. I went almost a full month, May, not doing it, felt great, told myself I felt great, and then rewarded myself, and now here we are, day after day after day after day, doing it again and again, feeling the same way I felt at the beginning of the year when I decided to stop all this.

In either case, that's done with. It's a beautiful day. Didn't get good sleep last night, had a little bit too much wine. But going to go on my seven mile run today, watch some baseball. It's a beautiful day. Work on Dugout Edge a little bit. Maybe hang out with some buds, friends, try and have a healthy day today.

I don't think there's anything wrong in letting go, splurging. In fact, the work hard play hard mentality is kind of something that I can understand and I can get on board with. I think that it allows you to — if you're just working, working, working, you start getting to this point where you plateau, but if you take rest, you let loose, I think that you can go a lot harder when you do go hard. But I don't know, maybe it's better to just be consistent level throughout, do the right thing. I don't know, it's a balance. It's a balance.

But in either case, still doing good work, it doesn't mean all is lost. If you could hear my tone of voice right now, it's extremely optimistic. Even though this journal entry started with a pretty somber critical note, I am a very critical person when it comes down to it, something that I've learned. I just want to continue to be harder on myself, but I need to also understand that my standards are extremely high — and even higher for myself than they are for other people.
