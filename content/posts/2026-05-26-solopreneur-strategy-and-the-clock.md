May 26, 2026

I'm still very conflicted, and I have a lot of decisions to think through around what I need to be spending my time on. I've already kind of determined that I want to become a solopreneur. I think it's determined — the skill set that's required, the skill set that it takes to become a solopreneur, I think really fits my strengths and my skill sets. And I wanted to do that, but I'm, you know, contemplating whether or not I continue working on Dugout Edge. And I think that this is gonna continue to change.

But there's also something about simply speaking to an agent 24/7 — just talking, checking, and reviewing — that maybe doesn't scratch the itch and doesn't fulfill me as much as I think I thought it would. But the act of creating, building a system, automating — that is extremely fulfilling to do.

I know that I need to start learning how to create on social media and market organically, and attempting to create videos. And me, I just don't feel that Dugout Edge is a product or an offering that will perform well on social media — but maybe it's just that I haven't tested it. Like, I think I need to be building out that social media workflow right now, where I can automatically create slideshows and video: how to be a better coach, how to coach baseball, how to teach baseball, especially for youth.

Part of me also thinks that I need an app to develop a product first. I guess start by building out the marketing channel first. You can go into those in sequence, in parallel to each other. Just learning how to gain the output. I don't need to build it from my own account. I can create multiple UGC accounts. Post slideshows. And then on the last slide, I just advertise Dugout Edge — download Dugout Edge to learn more, visit dugoutedge.com to learn more. I really need to be studying what a lot of these different UGC viral growth apps are actually doing, to figure out how they're doing it.

Another thing I'm really concerned about is, like, can I just live in the digital realm? Can I just code my way to a real company, or do I need to start learning to delegate? Do I need to start learning to hire, or sell, or speak to users? Can I continue down... what am I doing wrong? What do I need to be doing? I need to be honest, keep myself honest, and assess what it takes to create a product or a customer business.

Again, I think the biggest thing is, like, I don't want to waste time. I spent so much time building Dugout Edge to this point, getting it to where it is. Do I take the lessons from that and apply them somewhere new? Or do I just double down? It seems to be working, and I'm not even putting in that much effort into getting it to work. I'm hardly marketing it — it's just marketing itself on Google. Imagine if I started building out other surface area and other marketing channels. Another hard question.

There's just so much to think about, so much to consider. So many things I want to do. I want to save time to spend my life — like, go outside. I'm going to get in shape, learn new skills, learn a new sport, learn a new language. Gosh, there's just so much. And I also just want to be the one to go outside and play, learn a bit of everything. There is so much that I want to do for myself, and for my friends, and for my family. I just want to solve my own little problems. The thing is, is it just a hammer-and-a-nail problem? Can I actually solve the most important problems with technology?

I get the overwhelming sense that there's a clock that's ticking, and that I don't have much time left. Data practitioners at companies are being replaced by chat and code, and I think my time is limited.
