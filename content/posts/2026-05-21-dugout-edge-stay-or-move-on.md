May 21, 2026

I keep oscillating back and forth between work hard, grind now, delay gratification — and just enjoy life. Do the things that you like to do. Whatever it is in the moment that you want, do it.

So many ideas, so little time. I know I love playing with friends, getting outdoors, peaking my curiosity through learning about new technologies, thinking about new businesses. Dreaming. Dreaming is fun.

Then it comes down to what I want to work on. I know I just have to put the pedal to the metal, not think about the big shiny ideas and get down to the small minute details. It's difficult.

I'm in a place where I've built so much, never learned so much, and Dugout Edge is now my small child, my small baby — but it's still small. It's a small child that isn't going to make me a million. Or so I think. But maybe that's how it's got to be. I don't know. Should I be seeing more traction, less traction? Is it an idea that I want to keep pursuing, or is it time for me to move on?

What is it that I want? What is it that I want? Do I move on, do I stay? Do I move on, or do I stay?

The question is really eating away at me right now, and I'm not sure what the answer is. I think I've learned that I can build something. But what I'm afraid of is starting over. Nobody wants to start over. You spend one year at a college, you don't want to start over, you don't want to redo your degree, you don't want to lose those credits. But if it's not in the grand goal, if it's not in the roadmap, then it's sunk cost.

But here's the thing. My goal is financial freedom — getting the opportunities to work on whatever I want, not relying on a job for my income and my financial well-being. And Dugout Edge is supporting that financial well-being. But is it the project that's going to get me to where I want to go?

Do I spend five years grinding on Dugout Edge to get the $100,000 per year outcome? Or do I restart and spend five years on something else that has a 5% to 10% chance of giving me the $100 million outcome?

## Evening reflection — what came out of today

Spent the rest of the day actually mapping the territory instead of just asking the question. A few things crystallized.

**The thesis got real.** Content SEO is dying because of AI Overviews. Tool SEO is the new lead magnet — people search for software they can *use*, not articles they can *read*. Claude Code makes the builds fast enough that a portfolio of small tools can compound. Filter: deterministic output, LLM-unkillable in chat, in my domains, real search demand, pain proximity, and I want bigger ARPU bets not just $5/mo SaaS.

**The data surprised me.** Pulled keyword research across ~12 candidate clusters. The strongest single-domain SEO wedge in the entire portfolio is **fitness tools**. "1rm calculator" is 110,000/mo at KD 7. "Marathon training plan" is 74,000/mo at KD 12. The incumbents (Strength Level, Hal Higdon, ExRx) are old and ugly. The "best in class" position is sitting empty. And I'm in the audience — I lift, I cut, I'm GLP-1 curious.

**One real "if you build this, it works" candidate that wasn't on the list: an AI agent called Joe.** The idea: an autonomous AI that does the nickel-and-dime financial optimization humans are too lazy to do themselves. Calls customer service for a $5 refund. Drafts the complaint email. Burns down the Amex Platinum credits before they expire. Sends you cupcakes at 11:59pm on the last day of the cycle because there's $10 in Grubhub credit left. Take 20-25% of captured value, pay nothing if Joe doesn't act. The personality is the moat. Rocket Money is a brand; Joe is a character.

**FIRE calculator suite was the surprise winner from keyword research too.** "fire calculator" 18k/mo, KD 12, +124% yearly. "coast fire calculator" 27k/mo, KD 9, +83% yearly. And I'm literally weighing the quit-my-job decision right now — perfect personal pain.

**Personal finance for couples is mostly a trap.** Rocket Money owns subscription tracking so hard that 11k of 17k monthly searches in the cluster are *their branded query*. Honeydue/Zeta own couples budgets. The only opening in the broader finance space: medical bill negotiation (hospital bill negotiation 2,400/mo, KD 18, "ambulance bill negotiation" +127% yearly, no software incumbent). Combined with Mom's situation, this might actually be a real thread.

**What I keep coming back to:** the question I posed this morning — stay or move on — is the wrong frame. The honest answer is more like: build a small portfolio of tools that compound, with a much sharper thesis than I had this morning. Fitness tools as the SEO wedge. Joe as the brand-defining bet. FIRE tools as the personal-pain wedge. Maybe medical bill negotiation as the experimental one. Dugout keeps shipping in parallel — it's the cash flow that funds the experiments.

Less heavy than I felt this morning. More clear.
