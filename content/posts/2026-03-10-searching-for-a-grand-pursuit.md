# March 10, 2026

I've been thinking a lot recently about how I want to spend my days. While I'm very content with working a little bit on my computer, getting paid for it, working out, hanging out around the house, chilling every once in a while, going outside, hitting the driving range - I think I'm looking for a grander pursuit. And I think that is one of the biggest struggles that I'm facing today: what grand pursuit am I after?

Because when you have a grand pursuit, you know exactly what it takes to reach your goals. And by fulfilling those goals, you feel fulfillment. I liken it back to when I used to play baseball and my goal was to become a major league baseball player. Every second, every hour, every minute I spent working on my swing, watching baseball, perfecting my craft, was a minute, a second, a moment well spent because it was in pursuit of the grander goal.

And so what I'm starting to realize is that that grander goal is still missing from my life. That sense of adventure is missing from my life. While I have goals, I have wants and desires, there's no true north star for me at the moment. It's a little bit vaguer. I want to be financially free. I want to spend my days doing whatever I want. I want to get physically fit.

Now, some of these goals are quantifiable. I want to reach 12% body fat. I want to get to $200,000 in annual recurring revenue from side projects and hands-off income streams that I don't really need to manage or touch all that much.

At the same time, I think to myself, what is a life worth living? Is it spending your days in pursuit of a grander goal? Is it spending your time toiling away, grinding, sacrificing, giving everything you've got? Or is it spending your time a little bit more or a little less intentionally? Living moment by moment, enjoying things as they come.

I think about the best times in my life - now are amongst some of those best times, but time is moving way too quickly, which is telling me there's not enough novelty, there's not enough fun, and there's not enough adventures in my day-to-day. And now I don't know if that's just a function of where I'm at or getting older, but there does seem to feel like a fundamental piece that is missing.

I need to spend a lot more time with my own mind, sitting, thinking, breathing, meditating. I find that I really enjoy the process of meditating and journaling. It's therapeutic for me and it reminds me how to breathe, how to have good posture because I think that that is extremely important for me right now. And I think that a lot of the source of my pain is actually from not breathing right, from my body not being in alignment posturally, where it can breathe and relax with chest forward, neck back, shoulder blades back, spine tall.

Maybe what I'm missing is that social component of my day. I do spend a lot of my day in isolation, working with my computer. I get to practice speaking through daily stand-up and my discussions with AI, but it's not enough. I don't talk nearly enough.

Dugout Edge is going good, but I'm starting to see that if I don't expand in a meaningful way, if I don't approach it with some sort of new feature - a social media component - I don't see this thing growing to a point where it can replace my income, especially with the seasonality of it all. So of course I'm going to still continue to build, but I think I need to be smarter and I need to maybe think bigger. I need to think bigger, but maybe baby steps. It's just day by day by day, grow little by little and eventually you compound into a big snowball.

It hasn't even been a year, it's been six months and I'm at $300 MRR and honestly that's not bad at all. So I need to remind myself that I'm doing really well. But the funny thing is the better I do, the less I work. I get lazy, I get complacent and I'd like to remember that momentum begets momentum and I should continue working and compounding and snowballing this bad boy.
