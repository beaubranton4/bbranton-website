April 29, 2026

Today I got the opportunity to go to the Stripe Sessions Conference—fruits of Dugout Edge's labor. I got a free invite because I'm a Stripe customer and I guess have been doing well? I'm sure they don't invite everyone. It was crazy to see how many people turned out for this kind of stuff. I bet most got in for free, but many I'm sure also paid the $1000.

The conference was hosted by Stripe to discuss the internet economy, which is fitting because their entire product is and are the payment rails that enable the internet economy. Everything flows through them, all business, all commerce. There needs to be a transaction of value there. And the internet needs that verifiable trust layer to do that transaction, to act as the middleman between two hands, one hand with the money, one hand receiving money, and vice versa.

Got to hear from a lot of different tech executives, company founders, and just thought leaders in the internet economy space. There were many common themes, the most obvious one being AI and AI agents, agent commerce, agent payments. Those were the biggest takeaways for me—how the internet is shaping and changing from people buying things on the internet to agents buying things on the internet. And instead of agents buying from people, agents buying from agents. Micro transactions, purchasing via API, how payments are moving and shifting from moments in time. Building and selling for agents. Agents shouldn't be using the browser. Agents shouldn't be filling out credit card info. They need native rails.

Think about when you're looking at a product on Amazon: you're comparing options, you put it in your cart, you do a little bit of the "should I buy this? No, maybe I'll try this product. I'm going to actually buy something cheaper." Then you finally hit the checkout button, enter your payment details, buy the item. That's today's world.

So it's going from a moment in time to a policy. Payment is going from a moment in time to a policy. And by that, purchasing in the future, in the very near future—maybe not today, maybe not tomorrow, but in five, ten years time—is going to shift to a policy. "Please find me the best backpack you can find. I have only $100, no more. The less money that you can spend, the better. It must be black. It must have four and a half stars. It must be leather." It's a policy. And then your agent, your AI agent, will know your preferences, your policies, do the research for you and buy it for you. That's the future.

The coolest part of the day was when instead of Greg Brockman showing up on stage, Sam Altman made a guest appearance and was interviewed for one hour by Patrick Collison.

I really enjoyed going to Stripe Sessions because it was exciting and social. I felt like I was a part of something bigger than myself.

Another thing I'm realizing is that I need to switch up my environment. When I was in the Moscone Center, listening to the speakers, I had so many ideas. I felt so inspired. I felt so refreshed. Then as soon as I got home, I felt that fade. Back in my normal place where I'm lazy. That place where I just chill, hang out, procrastinate. And everyone needs that. But I need a place to go. To focus. To grind. To get inspired. To work. To pursue. To dream. And I think that needs to be separate from home.

Just to reiterate some things that really sparked my interest, in no particular order: optimization of token usage, agentic payments, data centers and optimizing those physical hardware stacks, using stablecoins, monitoring the market of agentic payments, payment streaming, and micro payments for small agentic services—things that agents want, maybe like data points, skills, certain things of that nature. Also MCP, ACP, the spikes in command line usage, API usage, free trial and token abuse. Those really stood out to me and I'd like to continue learning more about them.
