June 7, 2026

Today is going to be another good day of baseball. I watched an incredible Georgia vs Mississippi State Super Regional. Looking forward to watching UCSB vs UNC. Got that West Coast swag.

Yesterday had a great hike with Jess, Nick, Thomas, and Alana. Delicious Korean barbecue dinner, some drinks. Chill night. Fun to catch up with friends about, you know, the fun wedding that we just had in Hawaii.

The GameDay tool is really hurting me because it's just like — I don't know, I'm having a hard time making product decisions about what features to implement. How in depth to make the stat keeping tool. How in depth to make the GameDay tool. I think the scoreboard and the lineup board are all working. It's just that stat keeping piece — I've got three different modes and I'm not sure if I should just simplify it for the user. Keep one, make it one mode. We'll go all in. Full stat keeping just like GameChanger.

I suppose I can always build it out to be a more full tool. Just get that V1 out there, see if people like it, see if people are using it. First of all, I think my hunch is that since my primary audience and user base is age 6 through 12, they're not going to need a full stat keeper. They just want something simple to keep track of runs, keep track of hits, outs, pitches. They don't really need to know where kids are hitting the ball. But I think maybe it doesn't hurt to have that optionality, but then it just makes the GameDay tool a little bit complex, a little bit too confusing for some people.

Have a fun month ahead. Like I said, three trips planned — Truckee, San Diego, Toronto. I'm really looking forward to that.

But I think the theme for me, the key, is focus, discipline. Discipline and focus. It's just the two things that are super crucial and key to life in general. There's nothing wrong with having multiple interests, but if I really want to accomplish certain goals in certain areas, I just need to have one or two focuses. I can't be working on 10 projects at once. I've got to go all in on one. And compartmentalize — because if I have too much context switching going on, it'll never work out.

So here's to some focus. Here's to some discipline.
