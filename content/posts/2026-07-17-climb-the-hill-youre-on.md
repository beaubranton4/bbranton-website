# July 17, 2026

Today I woke up at a reasonable time, got my workout done and out of the way with. I did an upper body strength workout. I was really hoping that I would get a little bit of cardio in, maybe do more of a conditioning-based upper body workout with weights. I opted for a couple of supersets, seven exercises total, and got a good workout in still. I'm definitely prioritizing my exercise. I think I can do a better job of giving more effort when I go to the gym. While I do work hard and push my muscles, push the weight, I think I can be a little more calculated in the reps I do, the amount of weight I do, and stick to a more regimented plan so I can actually track my performance over time.

But maybe that takes the fun out of exercising. Going to the gym is definitely my fun, my decompress, my alone time. It sets the tone for my day, for my week, clears my head mentally. So maybe sticking to too much of a regimented plan is just the opposite of beneficial for me. In either case, I do think I can push myself a little more if I'm going to spend that time. Why not give 100%? But showing up at 80% on a consistent, daily basis is much better than burning out — giving 100% and then failing to show up.

I think the same can be said for my side projects, my pursuit of becoming an entrepreneur. Do I let myself take the easiest path, the path of least resistance? Or do I push forward, push upward, and climb an uphill battle? It's something I'm struggling to decide on a daily basis. When I log in and work on Dugout Edge, it's great that I'm showing up, it's great that I'm working on it — but am I really pushing myself in those moments? Am I working on the hard thing that needs to be worked on, the thing that's going to move the needle and push the business forward? Or am I just doing the thing that comes easy — prompting and typing and watching Claude go?

I know I need to make a change, but I'm just so comfortable where I'm at, and I keep telling myself that the change needs to be dramatic — that I need to quit my job so I can dedicate myself full time to entrepreneurial pursuits like Dugout Edge. But at the same time, that just might be an excuse — again, looking for an easy, quick fix, a one-time solution, and there's no such thing. I know that in order to make change, I need to change my behaviors, my daily routines, what I think about. And I think it all starts on the flip side of the coin — what I do outside of that, that will give me more energy, more motivation when it comes down to the time, the effort, the mental fortitude it takes to start something on my own.

While I've made great strides, my progress is slower than I'd like it to be. But everybody says entrepreneurship is a ten-year horizon — you're not going to get there in six months, not in year one, year two, year three. You have to keep pushing. But it's a constant mental battle of am I pushing on the right thing? I can work hard, push up the hill, but maybe I'm going up the wrong hill. Maybe I need to be picking a mountain. I might get to the top of this hill and say, wow, that's it — look at all these mountains I can see when I look up and over on the horizon, look how high they are, I'm so low compared to those. But I think I need to be where my feet are. I'm on this hill right now — might as well peak it. I'll grow my legs, I'll build up my stamina, and then once I'm finished with this hill, I'll walk over to the next one and climb that mountain.

Phrase of the day: get more juice out of the lemon.
