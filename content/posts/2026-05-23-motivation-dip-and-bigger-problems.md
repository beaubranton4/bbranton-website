date: '2026-05-23'

May 23, 2026

Just feeling really unmotivated to work on Dugout Edge at the moment. I think I feel like I've hit a wall where anything that I build out may not be that useful — the ROI might not be there. Like I'm starting to get diminishing returns, especially with the end of baseball season. A lot of little league seasons seem to be ending, and there just aren't as many people signing up. Then again, maybe it's just how it is, right? Your motivation wanes and waxes. I've been working on this pretty hard for the last four months now.

I know what I need to work on next. I can work on a game day tool. I can work on a mobile app. Maybe the game day tool needs to be paired with a mobile app because people won't have internet — I doubt many people will want to be setting up a hotspot or have an iPad in the dugout. People just have their phones. They're definitely not going to bring their laptop. So maybe I just emphasize those sorts of tools. There's also just working on improving the printables and building the tournament bracket creator, the schedule/league maker. These are all definitely areas where I can really double down and expand the business.

I don't know. Right now, the thought of working on those just doesn't excite me as much as building the practice planner or the lineup generator did. But I am still enjoying building out this software. So it's a bit of a conundrum that I'm facing in either case.

My mind is just starting to wander, thinking about other bigger things to focus on. Bigger problems, bigger industries. Robotics — getting involved in that space. Maybe Dugout Edge can build out some robotics, some pitching machines, ground ball machines, for baseball players. Could be an interesting one.

Helping with elderly — that's another interesting one. Especially now that my parents are aging, just making sure that I have the tools to take care of them, make sure that they're living a good life, and also just helping them out. Vision, memory, hearing — there's just so much that I think can be useful for them.

The next is just fixing social connections. People being lonely, hanging out at home, just being on their screens — like I'm doing right now, talking to computers, watching TV. I think that people just need to go out into the real world, experience things, meet people, talk to people, play with people, have fun.

So those are the kind of areas that I'm really interested in right now. And they all kind of tie to real problems, the physical world, my world. I just don't know what the very specific pain points are within those niches. I mean, I think I do know those pain points. I just don't know exactly what to build. And I think choosing something frightens me.

I feel like starting with some sort of wedge — figuring out where I can wedge myself in — is a good starting point.
