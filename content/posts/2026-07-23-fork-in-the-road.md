# July 23, 2026

More and more I'm starting to really feel that the skills I've developed in the corporate world are meaningless in the face of AI and AI technologies. I think AI and agentic capabilities have finally caught up — at my job, at least, we're starting to implement and put into practice the agents and technologies available to us in the world of data and analytics, which I think will make our lives a lot easier for a while. People will be able to really focus and hone in on the tougher, harder questions. Building the systems that can autonomously answer data questions, provide insights to people — I think those will be the in-demand skills. And unfortunately, I'm not really working on a lot of those projects. Those are the data warehouse team's.

Is it time to make a hard pivot, a career change, a leap of faith? Or do I double down, soak up the last remaining bits of juice while the getting is good? Either way, I ride it out, see what happens, let it take shape — but I need to always be in the back of my mind thinking about how I need to be looking for my next opportunity, and what that might be.

I'm like a senior at Stanford going into senior fall, not really knowing what I want to do. I got rejected from all the consulting gigs, so I tried to figure out what I wanted to do. Or rather — maybe I just got rejected from a return offer at CMA Strategy Consulting. Gosh, I really can't believe I got rejected by every single consulting firm. But hey, Disney picked me up, and I made it. I just don't feel like I'm necessarily made for the corporate world. I don't sell myself well. I don't come across as someone extremely intellectual until you really get to work with me. I'm a hard worker, decent at public speaking, decent at getting my point across — but I'm not exactly someone who jumps off the page, someone you can pattern-match to an excellent, A+ employee.

Just to re-emphasize the point about committing — I spent maybe an hour this morning just thinking about what I need to be thinking about for this next meeting with John, and how I need to incorporate it into my work, how I can get ahead. That's mental space I'd rather spend on something that actually moves the needle for me. I keep saying this, but I'm just being a pussy — too scared to take the leap of faith and truly commit to finding a new path. It's okay to not know the path you're going down, but maybe it's time to turn around, go back to that fork in the road, so I get the chance to make another decision, find a new opportunity, and explore what that might be.

I do really like building things with AI. I think I enjoy creating, researching, planning, and I feel like there are a lot of problems I can solve for myself — especially now that I'm an entrepreneur. If I can solve those kinds of problems for other businesses, other entrepreneurs, I think that's where money can be made. It doesn't necessarily have to be direct-to-consumer — it's extremely difficult, but doable too. Do what interests me. Do what I should be doing. What interests me — I should be doing what gives me joy and lets me have the most fun, because it's really hard to compete with someone who's having fun.
