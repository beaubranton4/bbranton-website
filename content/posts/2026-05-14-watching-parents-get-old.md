---

May 14, 2026

It's rough watching parents get old. I'm grateful that my job allows me to visit them for extended periods of time a couple of visits a year. It's a sad reality. It doesn't have to be that way. Some people design their lives around being close to family and prioritizing that. The common thinking seems to be prioritize money (your job), and it all seems so backwards.

It's so interesting that "money" and the concept of wealth — some intangible concept created by humans to represent value in our world — ends up shaping how almost everyone lives their life. Where they live, what they eat, how they spend their time.

I wonder if the ones who opt out of that world are the smart ones. The enlightened ones. But in this society, money is not optional. You can't really make it without money.

But there are levels. You don't need exorbitant amounts of money to live a good life. But there is a bare minimum.
