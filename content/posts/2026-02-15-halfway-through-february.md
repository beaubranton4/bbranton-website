February 15, 2026

Halfway through February. That's wild. Time just keeps flying, and I can feel it slipping through my fingers if I'm not intentional. It honestly feels like it was just New Year's, and now we're already mid-February. That alone is enough to remind me: I need to be laser-focused. More than ever.

I really do think focus is the key to everything. Not motivation. Not talent. Not even necessarily discipline on its own. Just pure, undistracted focus. Pointing energy in one direction and refusing to let it scatter. Time moves fast whether I'm intentional or not, so the only real lever I have is attention. Where am I putting it? What am I building? What am I getting better at every single day?

Yesterday was Valentine's Day, and it was such a good one. I spent the entire day with Abby, which is exactly how I wanted it. No rushing, no squeezing things in between work. Just presence.

We started the morning at a new Thai brunch spot that was honestly incredible. I love discovering places like that—slightly unexpected cuisine for brunch, but it worked so well. Everything was flavorful, fresh, just one of those meals where you look at each other halfway through and go, "This was such a good call." It's funny how much joy a really good meal can bring. It sets the tone.

After brunch, we went to the flower gardens in Golden Gate Park—the ones I had somehow never been to before. I've lived here for years and still haven't explored everything. That's kind of beautiful in its own way. There's still novelty in my own backyard. The gardens were peaceful, colorful, almost surreal in certain sections. Just walking slowly, looking at different blooms, taking it all in. No rush. No agenda. Just being outside, being together.

There's something about Golden Gate Park that always resets me. It makes San Francisco feel softer. Less hustle, more space. I think I need more of that in my life—intentional pauses in nature, even if it's just for an hour. Especially if I'm going to talk about being "laser-focused," I need to balance that with moments that refill the tank instead of draining it.

After the gardens, we came home and took a quick nap. That mid-day reset is elite. It's like you get two days in one. Wake up refreshed, slightly disoriented, but in a good way. I don't do that enough either.

Then we headed to Gray Area in the Mission. I had been curious about it, but honestly… kind of a scam. It just didn't live up to the hype in my head. Maybe I built it up too much, or maybe I'm just becoming more sensitive to experiences that don't feel worth the time or money. That's something I'm noticing more: I really value high signal experiences. If I'm going to spend time somewhere, I want it to either inspire me, teach me something, or genuinely entertain me. Otherwise, it feels like friction.

But even that was fine. Not every stop needs to be perfect. It's part of the day.

From there, we walked to a delicious dinner in Noe Valley. That's such a cozy neighborhood at night. The kind of place that feels almost suburban in the middle of the city. Warm lighting, quiet streets, little restaurants that feel intimate instead of flashy. The food was great, and it felt like the perfect close to the day.

Of course, we made a pit stop at Mr. Liquor in between—a very on-brand move. Grab a bottle, head home, keep the vibe going. There's something I love about those small in-between moments. Not the main event, not the headline, but the connective tissue of the day. Walking from place to place, talking about random things, laughing at nothing.

We ended the night watching a fun movie at home. Nothing groundbreaking. Just comfortable. Easy. Happy.

Days like that remind me why I work so hard and why I want to build something meaningful. It's not just about money or output or progress metrics. It's about creating a life where I can have entire days fully present with someone I care about. No stress. No looming chaos. Just choice.

And still, in the background, that voice is there: stay focused.

Because time is moving. February is halfway done. This year is going to disappear if I let it. I can either drift through it, reacting, or I can lock in. Build. Train. Write. Think. Execute. Stack days.

The goal isn't to eliminate days like yesterday. It's the opposite. It's to earn more of them. To design a life where those days are abundant, not rare.

Laser focus during the work. Full presence during the rest.

That feels like the formula right now.
