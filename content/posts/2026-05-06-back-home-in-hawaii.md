May 6, 2026

Back home in Hawaii. Been with my parents. Back home for Nick and Jess's wedding. Just so nice being back. Went for a run yesterday — just a two-miler, still kind of recovering from this, what I believe, is a sinus infection. But it's just the weather, the people, seeing so many people smiling, in shape, outdoors, running around, surfing — I just don't know if there's anything better than this. I don't know if anything beats where we live, where my parents live. San Francisco is a pretty close second, but I don't think anything can beat home.

I've been really bad about doing these journal entries over the last week or two. Just had a lot going on. One, feeling sick. I had Stripe Sessions a couple of weeks ago — or last week. Abby's birthday. So Friday, did Tia Margarita with our friends, which was a blast. Had way too many margaritas, spent way too much money. Good to see our friends. It's been a while since we all got together like that.

Saturday, I was deathly hungover. I don't know how I made it to her little activity with the girls in the morning, and then we drove up to Dawn Ranch, which is in Guerneville. Had a nice dinner at Sassy Mama's, a Southern barbecue joint, where the waitress or the owner, I think, had set up a special birthday table. But I didn't like how far in the back it was, away from the live music, so I asked to move us, and she didn't like that. But overall, it ended up being a good meal. The birthday dinner was pretty good. We had a nice, chill evening — s'mores, playing Code Names, watching *Beef*.

And Sunday, I pretty much just recovered. Walked through the town. Pizza, delicious pizza. And caught up with her parents for her birthday dinner. So that's been kind of the last couple of weeks.

Work-wise, got a new boss, or will be transitioning teams. Not sure how I feel about that. I do think it means I'll be working a lot more, a lot harder. But the run had to end sometime, and I still think I'm in a good spot, good situation. My base salary is still pretty good. Upside's a little bit capped, but what can I do about that? It's a good situation.

And plus, my goal is to become an entrepreneur. So I really shouldn't even be thinking about my career progression. Career progression is a fallback plan. I need to remember that.

Also need to remember that I *am* an entrepreneur. I really feel that the things that I'm doing — going to Stripe Sessions, that was a huge mental unlock for me. Seeing the word *standards*, *CEO*, and *impact*. Really helped me reframe how I think about myself. I'm at $20K ARR, and it's not huge, but it's further than I think 99% of people make it. So I shouldn't beat myself up, and this is not the time to rest on my laurels. Just the time to push even harder, press even harder. And continue building.
