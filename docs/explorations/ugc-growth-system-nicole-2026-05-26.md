# UGC Growth System — Nicole (@madebycol) on Starter Story

> **Verdict (2026-05-26): Keep — this is the execution manual for the viral-product thesis.** The [viral-product-brainstorm](./viral-product-brainstorm-2026-05-23.md) answers *what character/product to build*; this video answers *how to actually make it go viral and keep it viral*. Treat virality as a designed system, not a lucky video. Source: Starter Story interview with Nicole (founder of [Glam Up](https://www.glamup.ai/) + [Sprout](https://www.usesprout.com/)), [YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=32vqaJa90kw), 16:32, published 2026-05-17.

## The two notes that matter (Beau's takeaways, 2026-05-26)

1. **Build virality into your application.** Design the product so it's *innately viral* for social media — the app's surfaces (welcome screens, results, "scan your face" reveal) should produce shareable content by default. Distribution isn't a thing you bolt on after; it's a design constraint from day one. *"You need to think of the design of the app as part of your distribution."*

2. **Create a system for onboarding creators and coaching those who will advertise your app.** Don't rely on one-off posts. Build a repeatable machine to source → onboard → coach → manage UGC creators. Nicole's tailored creator *course* gets **>50% of creators going viral within their first 2 weeks**. The system is the moat, not the app features.

- **Glam Up** (women's makeup/glow-up): ~2M downloads, 1M users in first 6 months, peak **150K MRR**. Peak month: ~40–50M TikTok views + ~200M Instagram views.
- **Sprout** (formerly PrepearAI; job search for college / post-college): grew to **250K MRR in 8 months**, steady ~200K MRR. Hit **400M views in a single 7-day period**.
- **Two more apps in stealth**: ~200K MRR combined in 3 months using the same system.
- **Reach required:** went from ~100M monthly views (late last year) to **400–500M monthly views** across TikTok, Instagram, YouTube Shorts.

- Business model: **hard paywall**, weekly or monthly subscription. Buyer for Sprout = students/grads (higher purchasing power than the beauty audience — note this was a deliberate pivot reason).

## The playbook (step-by-step)

### 1. Find your viral distribution strategy
Test channels ~2–3 weeks each, "milk the hell out of" each one, iterate within a category before moving on:
- Reddit marketing
- TikTok / IG **faceless** content (slideshows, video, CTA variations)
- **Face UGC** — AI UGC or real creators
- (If you have capital) paid ads + influencer marketing

Glam Up's early engine was *faceless UGC*. Find the winner, then double down and scale.

### 2. Build the UGC creator system
**Prerequisite:** *you must understand your app and content better than your creators do.* Start by being your own creator, or onboard 2–10 creators and train them until they go viral, then systematize.

- **Sourcing** — find creators that fit the content format (talking-head → good talkers; reaction → good facial expressions).
  - *Inbound:* application forms in UGC group chats, Instagram, Reddit, or platforms like **Sideshift**.
  - *Outbound:* VAs doing outreach to micro-influencers.
- **Onboarding** — (a) *interview / vibe check* to confirm they'll take the campaign seriously; (b) a *tailored creator course* — Typeform + video modules + a **quiz at the end of each module** to quality-check completion. >50% go viral within 2 weeks.
- **Management** — options: hire influencers to manage creators, promote top creators to manage the rest, run a Discord, bi-weekly/monthly feedback calls. Find what fits your style.
- **Systematize & optimize** (once big enough) — referral system for creators, dashboards for growth/analytics.

### Core mindset
> *"It's really important to turn virality into a system and a process. It might be lucky at the start to get a viral video, but to maintain going viral, I don't believe it's luck… you can always maximize the surface area of your luck."*
