# Viral Product Brainstorm — Three Niches Through a TikTok/Comedy Lens

> Created 2026-05-23 — divergent ideation across the three niches surfaced in [baseball-training-robotics.md](./baseball-training-robotics.md), [elderly-care-tech.md](./elderly-care-tech.md), and [loneliness-social-connection.md](./loneliness-social-connection.md). Filter: which ideas have a built-in viral lever — comedy, character, or wholesome-cringe — that turns the marketing into the product.
>
> **Execution manual:** see [ugc-growth-system-nicole-2026-05-26.md](./ugc-growth-system-nicole-2026-05-26.md) — *how* to actually scale any of these to 100M+ views/mo via a designed UGC creator system (this doc picks the character; that doc runs the distribution machine).

## Verdict (2026-05-23)

**Three character-led products clear the bar.** Across 17 ideas, the only ones that pass both the viral-lever test and Beau's three-rule filter are products with a *persona* doing the talking, not a feature being announced. The Joe playbook keeps winning. Lead candidates:

1. **Story Capture** (elderly memory preservation) — grandma TikTok format + Beau's digital-twin engine + highest personal pain
2. **Bench Boss** (baseball) — anonymous AI manager character bolted onto Dugout Edge, becomes the marketing layer for the existing product
3. **Friend Audit + Hangouts** (loneliness) — Joe pattern applied to friendship; Beau IS the target user

**Notable pattern:** the three winners are all character-led, not feature-led. A quiz without a voice doesn't go viral. A coach without a persona is just another tool. Pick the character first, then build behind it.

## The viral lever differs by niche

| Niche | What goes viral | What dies |
|---|---|---|
| **Baseball** | Dad Coach comedy — universally relatable, low stakes, sweet | Earnest product demos, "look at our new feature" |
| **Elderly** | Wholesome > funny. Grandma content. Crying-on-camera reaction shots. | Comedy aimed at seniors. Sales-pitch energy. Anything that feels like exploiting decline. |
| **Loneliness** | Cringe-relatable + founder-led. AI roasting you. POV awkwardness. | Earnest "make friends!" messaging. Anything that admits the user is lonely directly. |

## Baseball — Dad Coach as a character universe

The audience already exists (Dugout Edge). The viral lever is taking the universal Dad Coach archetype and turning it into a character that lives on TikTok.

### 1. Coach Mode (AI swing/pitch roast)

Paste a video of your kid hitting, get a brutal-but-actually-helpful breakdown in a fictional coach voice. Hook: *"I asked an AI to roast my 9-year-old's swing."* Wholesome twist: it ends with ONE specific drill the kid can do tomorrow.

- **Funnel:** Coach Mode → Dugout Edge practice planner (the drill it recommends)
- **Filter score:** Pain proximity ✓, clear funnel ✓, passion ✓, deterministic ✓, LLM-unkillable (video analysis with a fixed-character output is hard to do in ChatGPT) ✓, ARPU bolted into existing Dugout Edge plan

### 2. Bench Boss (the character)

Anonymous AI lineup decisions delivered by a cigar-chomping AI manager. *"Sit Timmy. He's hitting .120. Sorry kid."* Removes the social cost of benching your friend's kid — the AI is the bad guy, not you.

- **The character does double duty:** lives inside the Lineup Generator AND runs a TikTok account that markets all of Dugout Edge
- **Content engine:** weekly "Bench Boss roasts your dad's lineup" submissions
- **Why it works:** Bench Boss is the Joe of baseball — a character with a strong voice and a single sharp opinion. Joe says "let me get your money back." Bench Boss says "sit your kid, he's not hitting."
- **Filter score:** clean across the board. Lowest infrastructure cost of all 17 ideas — it's a marketing layer on top of features already on the roadmap.

### 3. Dad Coach Tier List

Quiz: rate your dad coach archetype. *Yelling Dad / Stats Nerd Dad / Snack Dad / The Quiet Killer / The Former High School Star Who Peaked in 1998.* Screenshot-bait, share-bait, every parent group chat.

- **Funnel:** quiz → email capture → Dugout Edge soft pitch
- **Filter score:** great viral lever but weak monetization on its own. Use as top-of-funnel content, not a product.

### 4. Snack Mom Generator

Print-ready snack schedule with passive-aggressive notes baked in. *"Brenda, you have snacks 6/2. Last time it was Oreos. We're better than that."* Niche comedy snack moms will quote-share to their own group chats.

- **Funnel:** free printable → Dugout Edge upsell
- **Note:** there's already an archived `snack-schedule.md` exploration. This is the comedy/character-led version of that — different framing, same artifact.

### 5. Lineup Roulette

Generate the most cursed possible Little League lineup with a straight-faced AI justification. *"Putting your slowest kid leadoff. Pitcher in right field. Here's why."* Engagement bait that funnels into the actual Lineup Generator.

- **Filter score:** good viral, low standalone value. Lives inside Bench Boss.

### 6. Was That a Strike?

Phone-camera home-plate ump assistant. Travel-ball dads will weaponize this and the resulting TikToks (*"the ump missed 14 strikes this game"*) market themselves.

- **Filter score:** could be its own product OR a Dugout Edge feature. Polarizing — could actually make youth baseball worse. Flag for ethical hesitation; the comedy is real but the second-order effect on coaches/umps is bad.

## Elderly — wholesome content engine

Comedy is harder here. The lever is *emotional wholesome content* + the adult-child buyer persona. Grandma TikTok is the most-shared format that exists.

### 1. Story Capture ⭐

AI calls your aging parent on a weekly schedule, asks one question about their life, transcribes and edits the answer into a beautiful audio archive. Over months you build a navigable voice library of your parent's stories.

- **Viral hook:** each interview produces a shareable 60-sec clip (voice + captions + photo). *"I asked AI to interview my grandma about her first kiss. I'm not crying you're crying."* The user-generated clips ARE the marketing.
- **Reuses Beau's digital-twin engine** but pointed at parents instead of self
- **Buyer:** the adult child, not the senior. $30–100/mo.
- **Filter score:** clean — highest pain proximity Beau has across any idea in any exploration doc. His mom + dad are aging now. Building this would mean building a tool he himself would use this weekend.
- **Risk:** sentimental products are bought once and forgotten. Solve with cadence (weekly call), archive value (browseable library), and shareable artifact (the clip).

### 2. AirPods for Dad?

60-second quiz/calculator: input dad's hearing test results (or describe symptoms), get a verdict on whether AirPods Pro 2 ($249, FDA-cleared as hearing aids since Sept 2024) work for him or he needs real prescription aids. Affiliate AirPods + sequence into a hearing concierge for the cases that need more.

- **Viral hook:** *"I made my dad take the AirPods quiz. He failed."*
- **Category opportunity:** Eargo + Lexie merger (March 2025) left the DTC hearing space in flux with no concierge brand answering "should we just get dad AirPods?"
- **Filter score:** very good. Take-rate via affiliate. Solves a real adult-child confusion point.

### 3. The Mom Manual

AI compiles the operating manual for caring for your aging parent — meds, doctors, account passwords, important dates, neighbors' contact info, where the spare key is.

- **Viral hook (POV):** *"I'm 38 and just realized I don't know my mom's doctor's name."*
- **Buyer:** sandwich-gen 40–60
- **Risk:** sensitive data. Storage + privacy posture needs to be airtight.

### 4. Last Call (Joe for elderly care)

AI agent that handles the dread tasks for the adult child: disputes medical bills, cancels mystery subscriptions, refills prescriptions, calls the insurance company. Take-rate model — 20–25% of value captured.

- **Viral hook:** *"I let an AI call AT&T for my mom. We saved $2,400 in 30 minutes."* Same pattern Joe uses in finance.
- **Filter score:** strong. Joe sibling product. Direct overlap with Beau's existing Joe thesis.

### 5. AI Mom (post-mortem) ⚠️

After a parent passes, AI trained on the Story Capture archive answers your questions in their voice.

- **Viral hook:** controversy. TikTok will debate this to death.
- **Ethical weight:** heavy. Not a starter product. Mention as the long-term ceiling of Story Capture, not the wedge.
- **Flag:** do not lead with this. Story Capture is the build; AI Mom is the latent option value.

### 6. Tech Support for Parents (concierge)

Flat monthly fee (kid pays), AI + human handle all of mom's tech issues on FaceTime/phone.

- **Viral hook:** founder-led content. Hilarious recorded calls (with permission) of helping mom understand wifi. *"Mom called me because her wifi was off. The wifi was on. The TV was off."*
- **Filter score:** great pain proximity, less clean than Story Capture as a product — has a service-business shape (humans in the loop), which Beau historically avoids.

## Loneliness — cringe-relatable, founder-led

The viral lever here is **AI roasting you** + **founder-led documentation**. Lonely men in their 30s is Beau's exact cohort.

### 1. Friend Audit ⭐

AI reads your text history (with permission), generates the ghosting report card: *"You have 14 friends you haven't messaged in 90+ days. Here are the top 3 you should reach out to this week."*

- **Viral hook:** *"AI just exposed me. I've ghosted 23 friends."* Relatable humiliation = the most-shared TikTok format.
- **Funnel:** Friend Audit → "Want me to draft the reach-out texts?" → Hangouts (#2)
- **Filter score:** clean. Joe pattern. Take-rate harder for friendship, but $5/mo report + $20/mo Hangouts add-on works.

### 2. Hangouts

AI agent that schedules friend hangs. Knows your friends, knows their schedules, books the thing you've been meaning to set up for 6 months.

- **Viral hook (wholesome):** *"AI booked me a hang with my best friend from college. We hadn't talked in 4 years. We had the best night."*
- **Pair with Friend Audit:** audit identifies the gap, Hangouts closes it.

### 3. The Lonely Man Index

Quiz/calculator scoring yourself across 20 dimensions (texted a friend this week, ate alone yesterday, have a recurring activity outside work, etc.). Pairs with the AARP 2025 data (42% of US men report loneliness vs 37% of women — a complete flip from 2018 parity).

- **Viral hook:** the score itself is shareable. Earned media potential — Surgeon General data is in the news cycle.
- **Filter score:** good top-of-funnel for Friend Audit or Club OS. Weak standalone.

### 4. Run Club Wingman

AI picks the right run club in your city based on pace, vibe, gender-ratio preferences, schedule. Sends you a few low-stakes social prompts to use before you show up.

- **Viral hook:** *"I let an AI pick my run club. It chose 'Mid-Career Crisis Run.'"*
- **Funnel:** sequences into Club OS B2B once it exists (the club operator gets the back office tool; the runner gets the matching tool).

### 5. Last Saturday Night

Text a number Friday afternoon, get a curated weekend hang (event + 2-3 strangers also going) by Saturday morning. Timeleft model but text-first and activity-flexible (not dinner-only).

- **Viral hook:** *"AI booked me a kickball game with 4 strangers. We're now in a group chat."*
- **Filter score:** depends entirely on cold-start. Timeleft proved €18M ARR is possible with Typeform+WhatsApp+Stripe, but city-by-city supply is the unsolved problem.

### 6. ClubOS founder series ⭐

Not a separate product — this is the *marketing for ClubOS*. Document yourself launching a Hawaii surf club using your own product. *"Day 1: starting a surf club with 0 friends."*

- **Viral hook:** reality-show energy. Best-in-class founder-led content marketing — and validates the product publicly while you build it.
- **Why it matters:** ClubOS was already the strongest wedge in the loneliness research doc. The viral lever doesn't need to be a separate product — it needs to be Beau on camera, in Hawaii, building a club. The product is the protagonist's tool.

## Top 3 ranked across all niches

| # | Product | Niche | Viral lever | Filter score | Build cost |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Story Capture** | Elderly | Grandma TikTok (highest-share format on platform) | Clean — highest pain proximity Beau has | Medium (call + transcription + archive UI) |
| 2 | **Bench Boss** | Baseball | Character TikTok account marketing all of Dugout Edge | Clean — bolted onto existing roadmap | Low — marketing layer on existing features |
| 3 | **Friend Audit + Hangouts** | Loneliness | AI-roasts-you cringe-relatable | Clean — Joe pattern, Beau is target user | Medium (agent + text-history ingest) |

## The unifying observation

All three winners are **character-led, not feature-led.** Joe taught Beau this. Bench Boss, Grandma AI, and Friend Audit each need a *voice* with a strong opinion to break out — not just a quiz or a calculator. Before building any of them, design the character.

## Recommended next step

Pick ONE of the three and design the character. The cheapest, highest-leverage move: **Bench Boss**. It's the lowest infrastructure cost (the features already exist), the audience already exists (Dugout Edge customers), and a single week of TikToks would either prove or kill the character — without any product commitment.

The other two (Story Capture, Friend Audit) require building the underlying product before the character can market it. Bench Boss only needs the character.

## Open questions for Beau

1. **Story Capture vs Bench Boss as the first build:** Story Capture has higher personal pain. Bench Boss has lower opportunity cost. Pick by mood — emotional resonance or operating efficiency?
2. **Does Beau want to be on camera?** ClubOS founder series and Tech Support for Parents both require founder-as-protagonist content. Story Capture and Bench Boss don't.
3. **AI Mom ethics line:** worth deciding now whether that latent option ever gets pulled, or whether Story Capture explicitly disclaims it. Affects positioning from day one.
