# AI Data Analyst

## Verdict (2026-05-06)

**Real, growing category — but crowded.** Beau has a personal moat (lives the pain at UserTesting, knows the analyst workflow), and the search demand cluster is healthy. Opportunity is *not* "build another general AI data analyst" (Hex/Mode/Julius own that) — it's a vertical or workflow-specific AI analyst that someone like Beau understands cold.

## Competitive landscape

- **Hex** — notebooks + AI copilot; well-funded, dominant in startups
- **Mode** — BI + Visual SQL + AI; ThoughtSpot acquired
- **Julius AI** — consumer/SMB-friendly chat-with-CSVs
- **Hebbia** — enterprise unstructured data
- **NumbersStation** — enterprise
- **Claude / ChatGPT advanced data analysis** — generalist, eats the long tail
- **Cube + dbt + Looker** with AI layers — enterprise data stack

The general-purpose lane is full. The vertical lane is wide open.

## Where Beau has an edge

- Stanford grad + data analyst at UserTesting → understands the actual analyst workflow, not just the demo
- Already building (Dugout Edge) → product instincts
- Anxiety about being "replaced" gives empathy for the buyer who is the same person

## Possible wedges

1. **AI analyst for a specific industry** Beau knows: sports/baseball, in-person operations (ex-Disneyland), customer feedback (UserTesting domain). Vertical SaaS plays better than horizontal.
2. **AI analyst for solopreneurs / small Shopify+Stripe businesses**: most of them have no analyst. Bring the analyst in a box.
3. **AI for analysts, not replacing them**: workflow tool that makes one analyst as productive as five. Sells *to* the anxious searcher in the data above.
