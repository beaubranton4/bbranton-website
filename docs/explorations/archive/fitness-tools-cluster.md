# Fitness / Nutrition Tools Cluster

## Verdict (2026-05-21)

**This is the strongest single-domain SEO wedge in the entire portfolio.** The fitness-tools keyword landscape has:
- **Massive raw demand** (1RM calc alone: 110,000/mo; marathon training plan: 74,000/mo; macro calculator: 110,000/mo; body fat: 74,000/mo)
- **Absurdly low competition** on top terms — "1rm calculator" at KD 7, "marathon training plan" at KD 12, "macro calculator" at KD 31
- **Real commercial intent** — CPCs of $1–$3 across the cluster, even for "free" tool keywords
- **Programmatic SEO leverage** — every variant (1rm bench, 1rm squat, 1rm deadlift; marathon by goal time; macro for cut/bulk/maintain) is its own keyword cluster with its own volume

**You can absolutely build your wedge via SEO here.** This is the playbook of strengthlevel.com, runnersworld.com calculators, exrx.net — and none of them are doing the modern "Claude Code shipped in a week" version with a single sharp tool per URL.

**Recommended build order (ranked by SEO ROI):**
1. **1RM calculator suite** — 1RM bench, squat, deadlift, overhead press; KD 2–11; near-zero competition for the per-lift pages
2. **Marathon training plan generator** — input goal time + days/week + race date → personalized plan; 74,000/mo at KD 12
3. **Macro calculator for cutting/bulking** — branded variants ("macro calculator for weight loss" KD 23, 14,800/mo) are wide open
4. **Body fat calculator** — 74,000/mo but KD 35, higher competition; build as a "free" tool to capture top-of-funnel
5. **GLP-1 nutrition planner** — emerging niche, ride the wave (validate before building)

Each tool is buildable in 2–5 days with Claude Code. A site that ranks for 5 of these = 200k+/mo organic traffic in 12 months if SEO executes.

## Competitive landscape

The incumbents in this space are old, slow, and visually dated. None ship modern Claude-Code-quality UX:

| Tool | What they own | Why they're vulnerable |
|---|---|---|
| **Strength Level** (strengthlevel.com) | "1rm calculator" + percentile pages | Cluttered UI, ad-heavy, no shareable result links |
| **Hal Higdon** (halhigdon.com) | Marathon training plans | Static PDFs from the early 2000s — no personalization, no integration with Garmin/Strava |
| **Runner's World** (runnersworld.com) | Race pace calculator, training tools | Buried under content, slow, magazine paywall friction |
| **ExRx** (exrx.net) | Strength calculators, exercise database | Looks like a 2002 GeoCities page; SEO is pure age + backlinks |
| **Calculator.net** | All calculators ever | Generic. No fitness expertise signaling. Zero brand. |
| **MyFitnessPal** | Macro calc inside the app | Walled garden — search "macro calculator myfitnesspal" lands you on third-party SEO sites trying to capture the intent |
| **Healthline / Verywell Fit** | "What are macros" content | Content, not tools. AI Overviews are eating them. |
| **Cronometer / Carbon / RP Diet** | Branded macro/cutting programs | Paid apps; the free-tool funnel is unowned |

**The gap:** A modern, single-purpose fitness-tools site — every tool is the absolute best at one thing, beautifully designed, share-link enabled, instant result, no signup wall. Same playbook as crontab.guru in dev tools, just applied to fitness. Nobody is doing this in a serious way.

## Can the SEO wedge actually work?

**Yes — and here's the honest math:**

Conservative model for a single-tool page (1RM calculator):
- Target: top-3 ranking in 6–9 months (achievable at KD 7 with clean tool page + 20–50 quality backlinks)
- Position 2 CTR: ~15%
- 110,000 searches × 15% CTR = **16,500 monthly visitors** to just that one tool page
- Email capture rate 5–10% = 800–1,600 emails/mo from a single page

Compound across the cluster (1RM bench, squat, deadlift, marathon plan, macro calc, etc.) and you're looking at 100k+ monthly visitors within 12 months if execution holds. **That's a real audience.**

The risk is execution — generic calculator sites are everywhere, and you need to be the *meaningfully best one* at each tool (better UX, shareable links, modern design, mobile-first). Not a thin wrapper.

## Build playbook

**Phase 1 (month 1):** Ship the 1RM suite — single domain, 5 tool pages
- `/1rm-calculator` (home)
- `/1rm-calculator/bench-press`
- `/1rm-calculator/squat`
- `/1rm-calculator/deadlift`
- `/1rm-calculator/overhead-press`

Each page:
- Tool above the fold (no content gate)
- Input weight + reps → output 1RM + percentile + working weights for 5/3/1, Texas Method, conjugate, etc.
- Shareable result URL (encoded in querystring)
- Programmatic FAQ ("how accurate is the Epley formula vs Brzycki?")
- Email capture: "Get your personalized strength program based on your 1RM"

**Phase 2 (month 2):** Marathon training plan generator
- Inputs: goal time, current weekly mileage, race date, days/week available, optional injury history
- Output: full 16-week plan with paces (easy, tempo, threshold, intervals, long run) calculated from goal time
- Export to Garmin/Strava/Apple Health calendar

**Phase 3 (month 3+):** Macro calculator suite (cut/bulk/maintain, by activity level, by training goal), body fat calculator (multi-formula), VO2 max from race times, Hyrox percentile

**Domain:** something brandable like `fittools.app` or `liftmath.com` or `getstronger.app` — owns the modern-fitness-tool category.
