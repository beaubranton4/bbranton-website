# The Database of Me

## The Thesis

People don't want a million agents. They want a million versions of themselves working towards the problems they care about.

The future of AI isn't generic assistants — it's digital twins. Personalized agents that think like you, decide like you, and act on your behalf. But to build that, you need something that doesn't exist yet: a comprehensive, structured, machine-readable database of a single person.

This project is the first step toward that future.

## What This Project Is

beaubranton.com is not a blog. It's not a portfolio. It's a structured database of a person, designed from the ground up for AI training.

Every journal entry, every note, every decision captured here serves a dual purpose: it's human-readable content today, and training data for a digital twin tomorrow. The website is the interface — the real product is the data underneath.

## Data Sources

### Current

- **180+ voice-to-text journal entries** — Daily thoughts, reflections, decisions, and observations captured raw and unfiltered through voice dictation. These represent the most authentic version of how Beau thinks and communicates.

### Planned

- **Notes and ideas** — Fleeting thoughts, structured ideas, and creative sparks captured from Apple Notes and other tools
- **Reminders and to-do lists** — What matters enough to track, how priorities shift over time
- **Emails** — Communication patterns, tone, how relationships are maintained
- **Digital interactions** — Browsing patterns, saved content, bookmarks — the digital footprint of curiosity

### Future

- **AI interaction patterns** — Prompting style, response preferences, decision-making patterns when working with AI tools
- **Decision logs** — Explicit records of choices made and reasoning behind them
- **Feedback loops** — How the digital twin's outputs compare to what Beau would actually do, creating a self-improving training signal

## Phases / Roadmap

### Phase 1: Document the Vision
Plant the flag. Write down what this is and where it's going so every future decision has a north star.

### Phase 2: Structure Existing Data
Move from flat files to a real database. Tag, categorize, and create relationships between entries. Make the 180+ journal entries queryable and machine-accessible.

### Phase 3: Expand Capture
Open new input channels beyond journal entries. Email ingestion, notes sync, to-do list integration. The more data sources, the more complete the picture of the person.

### Phase 4: Build Training Corpus
Export and format the structured data for fine-tuning or RAG pipelines. Create the bridge between "database of a person" and "AI that acts like that person."

### Phase 5: Deploy Digital Twin(s)
AI agents that act like Beau — writing drafts, making decisions, responding to messages, tackling problems. Not one agent, but many versions working in parallel on different problems.

## Principles

- **Authenticity** — Raw, unfiltered capture. The value is in the real voice, not a polished version. Voice-to-text stays as-is, typos and all.
- **Structured for machines** — Every piece of data should be accessible to AI, not just readable by humans. Metadata, tags, relationships matter as much as content.
- **Version-controlled** — Everything lives in git. The history of how thinking evolves is itself valuable training data.
- **Privacy-aware** — This is deeply personal data. Access controls, clear boundaries between public and private content, and intentional decisions about what gets shared.
- **Capture over curation** — Volume and consistency beat perfection. A messy daily entry is worth more than a polished weekly summary.
